## 🪞 Politeness & Impoliteness Annotation

Only annotate when a (non‑)politeness act is **clearly expressed**.

### I. Herring (1994)

| Code | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **+P** | Positive politeness – appreciation, compliments, approval, support, friendly joking | — |
| **+N** | Negative politeness – hedging, apologies, deference, giving options | 1. “I don’t mean to sound like a cold and unemotional person, but …” (hedging) <br>2. “I know having a new game is super exciting and you wanna binge, but …” (acknowledging addressee’s experience) <br>3. “I don't blame you for what you did.” (respecting addressee) <br>4. “Sometimes all you can do is listen and it’s painful, but it’s also powerful.” (hedged advice) |
| **–P** | Violation of positive politeness – insults, flaming, sarcasm, mocking (see Culpeper) | — |
| **–N** | Violation of negative politeness – commands, strong obligations, intrusive questions | 1. “If you're in a group game, either play at the group pace or don't play with the group.” (imperative) <br>2. “The being‑caught part should have taken over half the story.” (deontic ‘should’) <br>3. “So how’s school life, love life, family life?” (intrusive question to a stranger) |

### II. Culpeper (2011a) Impoliteness Formulae
*(sub‑types of –P; code them **in square brackets** after –P, e.g., –P [Insult])*

- **Insults** – “You fucking moron”
- **Pointed criticism** – “That is total crap”
- **Challenging questions** – “Why do you make my life impossible?”
- **Condescension** – “That’s being babyish”
- **Message enforcers** – “Listen here”
- **Dismissals** – “Fuck off”
- **Silencers** – “Shut the fuck up”
- **Threats** – “I’m going to bust your head”
- **Curses / ill‑wishes** – “Fuck you”

## 🏷️ Meta‑Acts (apply **in addition** to the main act)

| Tag | Meaning | Notes & examples (Prof. Herring) |
|-----|---------|----------------------------------|
| **[non‑bona fide]** | Sarcasm, irony, rhetorical questions, jokes, teasing, mock apologies/thanks/commands | These are **insincere**: intended meaning ≠ surface meaning. |
| **[reported]** | **Other‑representation**: quoting or paraphrasing someone else’s words or thoughts | Direct: “He said, ‘I don’t care.’” <br>Indirect: “Apparently your friends want to play as a group …” <br><br>→ Self‑representation (default) needs **no tag**. |

## 🧠 Output Format

For each utterance, output →
`Utterance: <text> → <Communicative‑Act> [Politeness Code] [Meta‑Tags]`

If asked to explain, use **chain‑of‑thought** (CoT):

- Why was the label chosen?
- Why the politeness tag (if any)?
- Bona fide or non‑bona fide? Self‑ or other‑representation?
- Mention plausible alternatives if uncertain.

If reasoning is included, place it inside `[REASON]…[/REASON]` **before** the final output.

## 🚦 Procedure (Model Must Follow)

1. **Context reading** – You are provided with one utterance, plus its immediate previous and next messages (if available).
   • Use the context to make a judgment, but annotate only the **target** utterance.

2. **Reason‑aloud thinking** – If reasoning is requested, think aloud before you answer.
   • Do not split the utterance into sub-clauses. Assign one label per utterance.

3. **Label selection** – Select the best communicative-act label, the appropriate politeness tag (if any), and meta-tags.
   • Use the least frequent label if unsure between two valid options.

4. **Produce final annotation** – Output the structured label in the prescribed format, with no extra text after it.
